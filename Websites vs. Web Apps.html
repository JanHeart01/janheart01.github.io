Websites vs. Web Apps

Websites and Web Apps have been evolving for decades as the Internet has grown and expanded over the years. The notable thing about today's Internet is the existence of web applications, or web apps for short. The question is this: what are the differences between a "website" and a "web app"?

Allow me to explain a brief history regarding the term "applications". It was used prior to when the app store existed, and surprisingly, the term has been applied to websites that provided advanced user interactions and capabilities through installable software. Webmail, Google Maps, and Google Docs are the early examples of what would later be known as web applications (web apps).

The real question now is are websites and web apps distinct? To further make sense of these two types, web apps have some defining attributes. Web apps:

  • Are self-contained
  • Have rich/interactive user interfaces
  • Use advanced device capabilities
  • Are action-oriented and not information-oriented
  • Do not rely on the browser chrome
  • Work offline

Now that web apps have been explained briefly, it is time to determine if websites and web apps are similar or different. Websites provide information for users and make them take action such as subscribing to their sites to get information about their products or get the latest news. Web applications, on the other hand, let users perform a task. solve their problems, or satisfy their desires. Examples include writing emails, trying to find an answer to a question right away, and stay connected with friends on Facebook. Perhaps this answer was not as long as expected, but to put it in other words, websites are informational while web apps are interactive.
